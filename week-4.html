Week #4: New Tasks and Refactoring

Steven Jethro Vitangcul

Sat 05 July 2025 • 1 min read

My week for this internship was filled with opportunities to grow through new challenges. Another meeting, then new features again to work on for the automatic tester. I had to work on Saturday since there was a power outage in the university and in my home. I learned more on how the log files are generated and their content. I also learned a key in the configuration that manages the thread and shows as “Suspended” in the Task Manager. This made it easier to detect suspended programs in my code. Additionally, after so much debugging and help from www.regex101.com for visualizing the pattern, I gained a better understanding of how non-greedy and optional matching work together in regex. I then refactored my code so that it follows the DRY (Don’t Repeat Yourself) principle. I think I learned a lot this week especially about the software we’re working on.
